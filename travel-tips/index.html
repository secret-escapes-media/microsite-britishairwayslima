Must see: Parque de la Reserva

Opened in 1929 to commemorate the civilian reservists who fought in the War of the Pacific, Parque de la Reserva has become a romantic spot to get away from the bustling city. Its piece de la resistance is the Magic Water Circuit, a series of fountains with music and laser displays that has to be seen to be believed. Best caught at night for a romantic finale to a night out Lima-style.

Must do: Arequipa

Framed by three dramatic volcanoes, the second-city is a playground of Baroque architecture and stunning cityscapes. It’s culinary heritage is best tried at a communal picanteria restaurant – a must for any serious foodie.

Must go: Costa Verde Beaches

Lima’s beaches are perfect for nautical adventures or kicking back and catching some rays. There are plenty of activities on offer, with surfers riding waves all year round, and beach side restaurants for a variety of budgets.

Must eat: Rocoto relleno

A dish most popular in Arequipa, this Andean take on the stuffed pepper is a perfect lunchtime pick me up. Traditionally stuffed with a chopped beef filling, the rocoto pepper adds a generous amount of heat sympathetically quenched by a healthy serving of potato gratin. A must after a long day's trek.
