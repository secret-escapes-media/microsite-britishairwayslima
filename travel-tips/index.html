Must stay

Belmond Sanctuary Lodge is the only hotel located next to the ancient Inca citadel of Machu Picchu. It offers its guest easy access to the site and the magnificent gardens at the lodge boast stunning views across Machu Picchu.

Must do

Embark on a bucket list-topping trek to the lofty ancient ruins of Machu Picchu, shrouded in white cloud atop a high ridge in the Andes’ eastern slopes. Up here, you’ll be rewarded not just with one of the world’s most important archaeological preservations, but astounding views of the Sacred Valley below.

Must see

Cusco serves as a gateway to the famous ruins of Machu Picchu and the sacred valley. But Cusco in itself is steeped in history - with buildings dating back to the Inca and Spanish colonial times. Make sure you explore the capital of the Inca empire.

Must eat

Ceviche is the national dish; raw fish marinated in citrus juice.The acid in the juice “cooks” the fish, giving it a delicate flavour. The dish is usually spiced with red onion and aji pepper, and served with sweet potato or choclo, a white Andean corn.
