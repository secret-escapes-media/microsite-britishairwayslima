Trailblaze through epic landscapes

This vast land is something of a nirvana for adventuring types. Take on the dense Amazon for back-to-nature thrills on foot and over water, make for the coast to kayak and surf on the turquoise Pacific, venture into the Andes for mountain biking against a staggeringly scenic backdrop and lose yourself in one of many riotous Peruvian festivals.

If you're looking for an adrenaline rush, why not try your hand at paragliding and fly with the birds, enjoy spectacular views over the Pacific Ocean and Lima. Or if you would rather keep your feet firmly on the ground then you can sand board in Huacachina. This little town is desert oasis and playground alike, speed up and down the dunes in a buggy before taking to a board the to surf the dunes.

An extraordinary rail journey

Flying into Lima, you’ll be perfectly placed to board the recently restored train service to Huancayo – the world’s highest passenger railway and an engineering masterpiece. An unmissable experience, it climbs a breath-taking 4,829 metres in six hours, passing through a tapestry of green valleys, mining towns and glassy lakes.

The Inca Trail to Machu Picchu

Follow in the footsteps of the Incas by walking a 39-kilometre trekking and camping trail used by them throughout the history of their empire. The trail crosses through different ecosystems, colossal archaeological sites and terrain rich in flora and fauna, before arriving at the Machu Picchu citadel.
