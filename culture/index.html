Immerse yourself

The ancient and the modern exist side by side in Peru’s sophisticated capital. Explore the Gold Museum’s treasure trove of weapons and jewellery, dine at Huaca Pucllana for views over a major pre-Inca archaeological dig and join the throng in Plaza de Armas, with its ornate Presidential Palace and cathedral. The Lima of now is chaotic, creative and stylish. Urbane clubs and bars dot the city, contemporary galleries showcase new artists and its food scene is one of the most buzzed-about anywhere in the world.

Baroque splendour

The Monasterio de San Francisco is an ornate and intricate Baroque marvel in the centre of Lima. This Franciscan monastery boasts an impressive collection of some 25,000 antique texts, some pre-dating the conquest. It’s also home to cavernous and rather grotesque bone-lined catacombs – a must visit for added spook factor.

Lines in the sand

The Palpa Lines are a startling array of geoglyphs, and have stirred wonder, awe and mystery for generations. Older and more numerous than the wider known Nazca Lines, the Palpa Lines offer a glimpse into a strange and intriguing ancient world that is a marvel to behold.

Machu Picchu

Shrouded in mystery, the Inca citadel is an enigmatic symbol of pre-conquest Peru. Built in the 15th century, it was inexplicably abandoned, and was lost and forgotten for the entirety of the Spanish Conquest. Its buildings’ astronomical alignment, and sophisticated design, have enveloped the citadel in wonder; its purpose is still not clear. The views from here are some of the best in the world.
